The Child Specialist.

A licensed mental-health professional who focuses on the children's unique needs and best interests — a dedicated advocate for their well-being throughout the collaborative process.

The Role

What they do.

The collaborative divorce child specialist is a licensed mental health professional, typically a psychologist or social worker. The child specialist acts as a voice for the children during collaborative discussions, by focusing on the children's unique needs and best interests within their specific family. The child specialist does not act as an evaluator or therapist.

  • Understanding children's needs: helping parents appreciate their children's emotions and age-appropriate needs
  • Parenting plans: assisting in crafting tailor-made and practical parenting plans specific to the family's needs, instead of generic schedules used by courts
  • Professional consultations: consulting with other professionals involved with the children, such as teachers or therapists
  • Meeting with children: where helpful and requested by both parents, the child specialist may meet directly with the children

In short, the child specialist serves as a dedicated advocate for the children's well-being, helping to ensure that parenting decisions reflect the unique needs and best interests of each child.

The Advantage

One specialist, better outcomes.

In a court-focused case, each parent may retain their own custody expert, resulting in competing recommendations and adversarial dynamics that are harmful to children. In collaborative divorce, both parents work with one neutral child specialist. This not only avoids the costs of duplication but also promotes satisfactory post-divorce outcomes with a much reduced risk of further disputes — because both parents helped shape the plan.
