The Process

Cooperation, not courtrooms.

A collaborative divorce is a cooperative, out-of-court resolution process where both spouses agree to work together toward a fair settlement. Each person hires a specially trained collaborative attorney, but the focus stays on the parties — on crafting a resolution that works for them.

The process is multi-disciplinary. Depending on your family's needs, the team may include financial specialists, child specialists, and divorce coaches — each a neutral, each focused on their area of expertise. This approach minimizes duplicated costs and keeps decisions close to the people they affect.

The Participation Agreement

"If the collaborative process breaks down and the case proceeds to court, both attorneys must withdraw. This creates a powerful incentive for everyone to remain committed to resolution."

How It Works

Six steps to resolution.

Step 01

Separate initial consultations

Each spouse meets individually with a collaboratively trained attorney — to understand the process, ask questions, and decide whether this approach fits their situation.

Step 02

Signing the Participation Agreement

Both spouses and both attorneys sign the foundational document committing everyone to good-faith negotiation, full disclosure, and resolution outside of court.

Step 03

Building the team

Neutral specialists join the process — a financial specialist, a child specialist if children are involved, and a divorce coach to support communication and emotional well-being.

Step 04

Joint four-way sessions

Both spouses and both attorneys meet in structured sessions to share information, generate options, and negotiate agreements — at your pace, not a court calendar's.

Step 05

Drafting the settlement agreement

Once all issues are resolved, attorneys draft a comprehensive settlement that addresses every decision made — property, support, parenting — ready for both parties to review and sign.

Step 06

Court filing and finalization

The signed agreement is submitted as an uncontested divorce. No hearing is typically required — the court finalizes an agreement you helped create, shaped to your family's actual life.

Why Collaborative

A better outcome for everyone.

Collaborative divorce offers meaningful advantages over traditional litigation — in cost, speed, privacy, and long-term outcomes for the whole family.

01 / Less Conflict

Cooperation over courtrooms

The process focuses on problem-solving rather than adversarial proceedings, reducing emotional strain for both spouses and, especially, their children.

02 / More Control

Your decisions, your outcome

You and your spouse make the decisions about finances, property, and parenting — not a judge who doesn't know your family.

03 / Faster & Less Costly

No court calendar to wait on

Without lengthy hearings and duplicated experts, the process is typically quicker and significantly less expensive than traditional litigation.

04 / Private

Kept out of the record

Meetings happen in private settings, not open court. Personal and financial matters remain confidential throughout.

05 / Co-Parenting

A foundation for what's next

The respectful, team-based approach helps preserve the communication and cooperation that co-parents will need for years to come.

06 / Tailored

Agreements shaped to your life

Craft flexible, creative solutions that fit your family's specific needs rather than relying on rigid, one-size-fits-all legal defaults.

Your Team

Neutral experts, shared purpose.

In a collaborative divorce, specialists work as neutrals for both spouses — one financial expert, one child specialist, one coach — instead of each side hiring their own. It keeps costs down, reduces conflict, and leads to better decisions.

The Divorce Coach

Emotional & communication guide

A neutral mental-health professional who helps both spouses manage the emotional and communication side of the divorce — keeping the process on track without providing therapy or legal advice.

  • Improves communication between spouses
  • Helps manage anger, fear, or grief
  • Keeps meetings focused and productive
  • Prepares clients for important conversations
  • Protects the process from emotional breakdown
Learn more →

The Financial Specialist

Neutral financial professional

A trained neutral who supports both spouses and their attorneys with objective financial analysis — helping build settlements that are equitable, sustainable, and aligned with both parties' goals.

  • Gathers and organizes all financial data
  • Analyzes income, expenses, and cash flow
  • Models settlement scenarios and projections
  • Explains tax implications of each option
  • Coordinates with CPAs and valuators as needed
Learn more →

The Child Specialist

A voice for the children

A licensed mental-health professional who focuses on the children's unique needs and best interests — crafting tailor-made parenting plans instead of generic court-ordered schedules.

  • Helps parents understand children's needs
  • Crafts practical, tailored parenting plans
  • Consults with teachers and therapists
  • Meets with children when helpful
  • Promotes better long-term outcomes
