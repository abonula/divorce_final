The Financial Specialist.

A trained neutral who supports both spouses and their attorneys with objective financial analysis — helping build settlements that are equitable, sustainable, and aligned with both parties' goals.

The Role

What they do.

The financial specialist is a trained collaborative divorce expert who serves as a neutral, unbiased financial professional supporting both spouses and their attorneys. They provide objective analysis and financial insight to help the parties reach well-informed solutions. Their involvement helps ensure that both spouses and attorneys have access to comprehensive financial data.

  • Financial data collection: gathering and organizing information including investments, retirement accounts, business interests, real property, and debt obligations
  • Income and expense analysis: analyzing income, expenses, and cash flow to identify the short-term and long-term financial needs of each spouse
  • Settlement options: developing and evaluating financial settlement scenarios with projections illustrating the potential impact of each option
  • Tax implications: providing tax information to help both spouses avoid unintended financial consequences
  • Coordination: working with CPAs, business valuators, realtors, and mortgage loan officers as needed to provide complete clarity

The financial specialist assists the parties in developing settlements that are equitable, sustainable, and aligned with both of their financial goals.

The Advantage

One expert, no duplication.

In a court-focused case, each party typically retains their own financial expert — resulting in two sets of fees, two competing analyses, and disputes over whose numbers are correct. In collaborative divorce, both parties work with one neutral financial specialist. This eliminates duplication costs, reduces adversarial dynamics, and produces a shared financial picture that both spouses can trust.
